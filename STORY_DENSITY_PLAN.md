# Story Density Plan

## Purpose
This document keeps The Last Token's 45-60 minute expansion emotionally and narratively dense without turning it into a lore dump.

The game should feel richer through short lines, reactive NPCs, changing object text, and clear puzzle payoffs.

## Story Density Rules
- Keep lines short.
- Put lore in interactions, not paragraphs.
- Every required puzzle must teach one story idea.
- Every completed puzzle must have a return-to-story dialogue beat.
- Repeat dialogue should change by story phase.
- Optional lore should reward curiosity, not block understanding.
- Player confusion should be clear, but not verbose.

## Core Emotional Throughline
The player begins with simple confusion:
> "I know this place. I do not know why."

The player moves through contradiction:
> "They remember me differently."

The player reaches pressure:
> "The door is listening to more than one version of me."

The player reaches restoration:
> "I was the missing staff member."

Every quest should move the player one step along that line.

## Memory Signal Story Use

### Grounded
Story focus:
- Where am I?
- Why does Mira know me?
- Why does Cabinet 07 react to me?

Dialogue density:
- Simple, direct confusion.
- NPCs sound casual, which contrasts with the player.
- Machines speak in clear diagnostics.

Required story beat:
- Lost Token is recovered.

### Uneasy
Story focus:
- The arcade is reacting personally.
- Records are starting to identify the player.
- NPCs know more than they say.

Dialogue density:
- NPCs mention old routines and missing staff lightly.
- Mr. Byte takes the lead through machine records.
- Player asks more specific questions.

Required story beat:
- Truth Filter reveals contradictions.

### Fractured
Story focus:
- Memory and records disagree.
- The player is not only recovering memory; the arcade is restoring a profile.
- Multiple systems refer to the player indirectly.

Dialogue density:
- Vendo jokes about labels, signals, and returns.
- Gus gives practical hints that sound normal until reread.
- Optional echoes become more unsettling.

Required story beats:
- Circuit Soda stabilizes signal flow.
- Maintenance Sync teaches two-signal alignment.

### Overloaded
Story focus:
- The arcade is pushing the truth forward.
- The Staff Door recognizes the restored signal.
- The player notices fragments directly.

Dialogue density:
- Fewer jokes.
- Shorter, sharper lines.
- Player observations become more intimate.

Required story beat:
- Staff Corridor and Memory Echo lead to reveal.

### Restored
Story focus:
- Employee 04 can be named.
- NPCs can stop avoiding the truth.
- Optional content can resolve emotionally.

Dialogue density:
- Clear post-reveal acknowledgements.
- No need to over-explain the entire backstory again.
- Let small objects carry emotional detail.

Required story beat:
- Ending / post-reveal roam.

## Required Puzzle Story Jobs

| Puzzle | Story Job | What Player Learns |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Rockbyte Duel | First machine confrontation | A machine holds something that belongs to the player |
| Truth Filter | Contradiction test | Records and memory do not agree |
| Circuit Soda | Signal routing | Identity can be mislabeled and rerouted |
| Maintenance Sync | Two-signal stabilization | The Staff Door needs both versions/signals |
| Memory Echo | Threshold reflection | The missing staff identity is close |
| Staff Room Reveal | Truth | The player is connected to Employee 04 |

## Dialogue Per Quest

### Lost Token
Before:
- Mira recognizes the player.
- Player does not recognize themselves clearly.

During:
- Cabinet 07 treats the player like an incomplete signal.

After:
- Mira says one memory came back.
- The next lead points to machine records.

Example completion beat:
> Mira: "That token was yours. Or it remembered you like it was."
> Player: "Why does that make me feel worse?"

### Truth Filter
Before:
- Mr. Byte says the arcade has conflicting records.
- Player asks why the machines keep talking like they know them.

During:
- Statements contradict prior dialogue and visible hub facts.

After:
- Mr. Byte confirms the contradiction is readable.
- Player realizes memory is no longer the only witness.

Example completion beat:
> Mr. Byte: "Truth Filter passed. Contradiction preserved."
> Player: "Preserved?"
> Mr. Byte: "Deleted truths return incorrectly."

### Circuit Soda
Before:
- Vendo mocks the player's unstable signal.
- The puzzle is framed as routing a mislabeled input.

During:
- The player matches unstable signals to correct outputs.

After:
- Vendo jokes, then gives a real clue toward Maintenance Hall.

Example completion beat:
> Vendo: "YOUR SIGNAL NOW HAS 12% LESS IDENTITY SLUDGE."
> Player: "That was almost helpful."
> Vendo: "NO REFUNDS."

### Maintenance Sync
Before:
- Gus explains the Staff Door listens for two stable signals.
- He makes it sound like a maintenance problem.

During:
- The player aligns two inputs before they decay.

After:
- Gus says the door heard both versions.

Example completion beat:
> Gus: "There. Door heard both of you."
> Player: "Both?"
> Gus: "Yep. Bad sentence. Useful result."

### Memory Echo
Before:
- The player enters a quieter, more pressured space.

During:
- Short first-person observations.
- No large exposition blocks.

After:
- Staff Room reveal begins.

Example beat:
> Player: "The hall is shorter than I remember."
> Player: "I do not remember remembering it."

### Staff Room Reveal
Before:
- Systems identify the player more directly.

During:
- Memory panels connect prior objects and NPC lines.

After:
- Post-reveal roam unlocks.

Example beat:
> Player: "Employee 04."
> Player: "That was not a clue."
> Player: "It was my name tag."

## Optional Story Density

### Broken High Score
Use:
- Corrupted records.
- Previous score / blank name.
- Optional Roxy commentary.

Should not:
- Reveal Employee 04 fully.
- Become required for main route.

### Prize Counter Secret
Use:
- Small personal memory.
- Token/prize connection.
- Pip noticing details others ignore.

Should not:
- Add inventory complexity.
- Require backtracking across many maps.

### Owner Portrait Chain
Use:
- Shifting nameplate details.
- "0 4" foreshadowing.
- Post-reveal confirmation.

Should not:
- Count as required proof for the ending.

### Broken Cabinet Interactions
Use:
- Short corrupted echoes.
- Machine fragments.
- Tone reinforcement.

Should not:
- Launch another required game.

## Repeated Dialogue Rules
For each story phase:
1. First talk can provide flavor or new information.
2. Second talk can vary the flavor.
3. Third and later talks should nudge the player toward the current objective.

Tone by character:
- Mira: gentle nudge.
- Gus: dry practical nudge.
- Vendo: sarcastic nudge.
- Mr. Byte: diagnostic nudge.
- Roxy: competitive nudge.
- Pip: curious nudge.

## Object Text Rules
Object interactions should change by Memory Signal when useful.

Example Owner Portrait progression:
- Grounded: "The portrait is too scratched to read."
- Uneasy: "The frame looks recently disturbed."
- Fractured: "Only two marks are readable. 0 4."
- Overloaded: "The nameplate almost holds still."
- Restored: "Employee 04. The letters no longer hide."

## Avoiding Lore Dumps
Instead of one long explanation:
- Put one clue in an NPC line.
- Put one clue in a puzzle rule.
- Put one clue in an object interaction.
- Put one clue in a post-completion reaction.

The player should assemble the story through repeated small confirmations.

## QA Questions
Use these before accepting any expanded story content:
- Does this beat point to the next objective?
- Does this line reveal one useful thing, not five?
- Does the puzzle return to story afterward?
- Does the owner NPC matter?
- Does this preserve the Employee 04 reveal?
- Does optional content stay optional?
- Does save/load preserve the story phase?

If a scene or quest fails these questions, trim it before expanding it.
