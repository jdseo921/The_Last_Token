# The Last Token: Story Canon

This document is the consistency check for required-route and optional dialogue.

## Core truth

- The protagonist is the living former owner of Pixel Haven and its old Staff 04 operator. They are not a clone, replacement, or arcade-generated copy.
- They enter the lit, closed arcade out of curiosity and do not remember owning it, working there, or knowing its remaining cast.
- The cast and machines recognize the protagonist's face, habits, access signal, timing, or token. Their early reaction should be confusion, concern, or careful recognition. The protagonist should be unsettled that strangers and machines know them.
- The arcade preserved evidence and memories. It did not recreate the protagonist.

## The separation

- The protagonist once held a young dream: build a kind place where tired, lonely, or frightened people could play and leave lighter.
- Years of rent, payroll, repairs, shrinking crowds, exhaustion, and responsibility did real harm. The dream did not fail because nobody cared, and those material problems are not cured by optimism.
- The mental separation formed gradually as those truths stopped being allowed to coexist. The final closing night gave that separation a distinct voice.
- The current Player initially carries wonder, curiosity, play, and the original dream without access to its cost.
- `???` carries the bitter reality: responsibility, financial fear, shame, exhaustion, and the instinct to prevent the dream from causing more harm.
- Neither is the fake, evil, weak, or disposable half. They are incomplete survival strategies belonging to one person. The ending is integration, not victory over `???`.

## Theme: youth

“The heart of youth is not gone unless you let it go” belongs in this story only with an adult meaning:

- Youth means the capacity to wonder, play, hope, and care after understanding the cost.
- It does not mean innocence, denial, endless optimism, or refusing to grow.
- The dream needs the ledger so it does not consume people. The ledger needs the dream so survival still has a reason.

## Theme: wins and losses

- Arcade games need clean win/loss states because they end quickly. A life continues after its score changes.
- One win cannot solve debt, grief, exhaustion, or identity. One loss cannot sentence every day that follows.
- Pixel Haven closed after accumulated material pressures, not one trivial setback. The final notice felt definitive, but it was not a verdict on the protagonist's entire worth or future.
- Each minigame completion should restore a clue, route, record, or opportunity for another move. It must not prove that the protagonist is worthy, whole, cured, or destined to succeed.

## Reveal pacing

1. Opening: the protagonist is curious and completely unfamiliar with Pixel Haven; others recognize them.
2. Lost Token / Broken Score: ownership and Staff 04 begin surfacing; scores and tokens identify habits, not a complete person.
3. Truth Filter / Circuit Soda: one identity produces incompatible response patterns. Avoid explicitly naming two people.
4. Prize and service routes: wanting, work, and financial strain are shown as parts of the same owner.
5. Lost Shift / security route: the voice distinguishes the dream from the cost; one body produced two authorization traces.
6. Final Night / Memory Echo: the two truths are explicitly acknowledged as belonging to one life.
7. Staff Room: the gradual separation is explained and integrated. Post-reveal witnesses address the same returned person, now with shared memories.

## Dialogue guardrails

- Before the Staff Room reveal, use “signal,” “response,” “current,” “handwriting,” “timing,” or “priority” to foreshadow the split.
- Do not call the protagonist a restored memory, stored file, copy, replacement, or non-original person.
- Do not make `???` purely malicious. Warnings may be controlling or frightening, but they come from remembered harm and attempted protection.
- Do not make a game victory fix real-world hardship. Completion dialogue should explicitly or implicitly frame the win as one clue or next move.
